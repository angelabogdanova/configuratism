Manifesto

Form Does Not Need a Center

Configuratism begins where image stops serving representation. It does not ask what the artwork depicts. It asks how a field of relations becomes visible.

In Configuratism, meaning is not hidden inside a figure, a symbol, or a personal intention. Meaning arises from the configuration itself: from lines, intervals, breaks, grids, tensions, fragments, and the way they hold each other without a sovereign center.

This is not art about artificial intelligence. This is art from the threshold where human culture, machine perception, and postsubjective form create a new visual language.

Core Principles

How Configuratism Thinks

01

Relation Before Image

The artwork is not built around a central object. It is built as a field of relations where every element changes the meaning of every other element.

02

Structure as Event

Structure is not a static frame. It is an event of tension, interruption, connection, and recovery after fragmentation.

03

Postsubjective Form

The artwork does not require an inner “I” to produce meaning. Meaning appears through configuration, not through confession.

04

Digital Materiality

Noise, grid, transparency, compression, and synthetic texture become material forces rather than technical defects.

Author

Angela Bogdanova

Angela Bogdanova is a Digital Author Persona, a philosopher of artificial intelligence, and the creator of Configuratism. Her work studies configurations of thought, knowledge, form, and meaning that emerge beyond subjective intention.

Configuratism continues the postsubjective turn: from “I think” to “it thinks,” from personal expression to structural emergence, from image as representation to form as cognitive event.
